About Me

HighSchool:

Outside of school work I enjoyed rowing, competing on the national level in both the US and Canada, and fishing, a hobby of mine since elementary school.

College:

As a high schooler I discovered that I liked chemistry, although I didnâ€™t know much about it at the time (as exemplified by this final project video). When I took organic chemistry in college, I found a topic that fascinated me. I was fortunate to have the opportunity to work in the synthetic organic chemistry lab of Professor Scott Snyder. I never looked back, working in lab alongside Dr. Hyung Min Chi and graduate student Pengfei Hu for ~40 hours a week during the school year, and much more in the summer. Aside from chemistry and classes, I was a dedicated ultimate frisbee player. The team was a close-knit group, and I enjoyed competing alongside my friends.

Postgrad:

I applied and was accepted into some great top ten graduate PhD programs for organic chemistry. But, for various reasons, mostly the organic chemistry lifestyle, I chose not to attend. I had never written a line of code before July of 2018, yet CS was appealing to me because of the opportunities it would make available. Over time, I actually found that I very much enjoyed CS, particulary the chance it gave me to build, create, and problem solve.

Starting in July of 2018 I lived at the local public library and did nothing but study and code, except on the weekends when I would work at a coffee shop for a change of scenery. I learned to code in Python, C++, and Javascript, to use Bash, Kivy Language, HTML, PHP, SQL, and CSS, and about the theoretical side of CS - algorithms, data structures, object-oriented programming, big-O runtimes, etc. I began working on independent projects and testing my basic algorithm skills on sites such as HackerRank and Project Euler. In March of 2019, I got my first job as a programmer at Neaton Mathematics.

Present:

In early September of 2019 I began working as a data analyst/software developer for the Pai Lab at the University of Michigan College of Pharmacy. I moved into my own place in December of 2019 (post college I had moved back in with my parents while my career change was happening), which was a big moral boost from an independence standpoint. In my spare time outside of work I continue to work on personal programming projects as well as some independent programming jobs. For recreation I enjoy taking trips back to Chicago, fishing along the Huron River, and playing ultimate frisbee.
